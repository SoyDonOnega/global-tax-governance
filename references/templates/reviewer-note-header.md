# REVIEWER NOTE HEADER

> Mandatory block at the top of every substantive workspace output (memos, papers, dashboards, review tables, board reports, AIAs).
> Philosophy: the output is structure · the decision is the lawyer's · every cell is a lead, not a finding.

---

## TEMPLATE — paste at the top of every deliverable

```
─────────────────────────────────────────────
REVIEWER NOTE
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Generated         : [YYYY-MM-DD] · [skill / flow used]
Client / matter   : [matter or "practice-level"]
Sources consulted :
  · [norm / article / DGT ruling / judgment with ECLI]
  · [doctrine with verified cite — DOI / page]
  · [secondary sources if applicable]
Reading coverage  :
  · [what was read in full vs. partially vs. summary]
  · [documents not consulted that would be relevant]
Open flags        :
  · [⚠ areas of normative uncertainty]
  · [⚠ aggressive positions or with negative TEAC/TS precedent]
  · [? points pending client confirmation]
Currency check    : [last verification of tax-currency-watch.md — YYYY-MM-DD]
Before acting     :
  · [verify X against primary source]
  · [contrast Y with recent DGT binding ruling]
  · [senior review before sending to client / publishing]
─────────────────────────────────────────────
```

---

## WHEN MANDATORY

- Technical memos for clients
- Academic papers (adapted version: verified sources, gaps, methodological notes)
- Interactive dashboards
- Review tables / tabular reviews
- AI Impact Assessments (AIA)
- Board / audit-committee reports

## WHEN OPTIONAL

- Operational conversation (not a deliverable)
- Code snippets
- Internal drafts under `Research/`

## HARD RULE

A substantive output delivered **without** a reviewer note is defective. Absence of the block equates to presenting a finding without evidence.
